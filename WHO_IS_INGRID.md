# Who Is Ingrid — and Why This Project Exists

This document exists because the VIZZ project raises a question worth answering directly:

**Who builds a production AI system, for free, over seven months, for a plastic surgery clinic?**

## Why Unpaid

This is a question Ingrid gets asked.

The honest answer is that this project sits at the intersection of several things she cares about deeply:

**Healthcare access in Brazil.** The patients VIZZ talks to are often people who have been wanting a procedure for years but didn't know how to navigate the process, didn't feel confident asking questions, or simply got lost in a sea of unanswered WhatsApp messages. VIZZ gives them an immediate, informed, respectful response. That matters.

**The research.** All the conversations VIZZ generates are data. Data about how patients make decisions about elective surgery, what makes them drop off, what questions they ask first, what payment methods they prefer, how long it takes from first contact to payment. This feeds a Health Economics research project on no-show prediction — a real academic problem with real clinical consequences.

**The proof of concept.** Ingrid wants to understand, in practice, what it means to build production software with AI tools without programming knowledge. VIZZ is that experiment. The fact that it works — with real patients, real money, real medical stakes — is the data point.

**The relationship.** Dr. Vizzotto and his team trust Ingrid with something important: the first contact their patients have with the clinic. That trust is not taken lightly. The work continues because the responsibility is real.

## What Ingrid Actually Does

On any given week, Ingrid:

- Reads through real patient conversations to find where VIZZ felt robotic or missed the tone
- Translates those observations into behavioral instructions and updates the system prompt
- Tests new features by sending messages to VIZZ herself
- Monitors the conversation database for bugs, edge cases, or patterns worth addressing
- Works with Fran to understand what's happening operationally and whether VIZZ is serving those needs
- Contributes to the academic research design around the data VIZZ generates

She does not write code. She writes requirements, observes behavior, and provides feedback — the same skills a domain expert brings to any software project, but now applied directly to AI that can execute.

## What This Project Is Evidence Of

Ingrid's background is in health economics, not technology. But she could build this because the tools have changed.

The limiting factor in building software used to be technical skill. Now the limiting factor is **clear thinking about what you want to build and why**.

Domain expertise — understanding patients, understanding clinical workflows, understanding what "humanized" actually means in this context — turned out to be the most valuable input into the system. The AI handled the rest.

This is not a story about technology replacing expertise. It's a story about expertise finally being able to express itself directly, without being filtered through an intermediary who doesn't fully understand the domain.

VIZZ works because Ingrid understands patients. Not because Ingrid understands code.
